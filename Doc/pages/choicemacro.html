The <<choice>> Macro

The <<choice>> macro locks away paths in your story. A choice looks exactly like a regular link, but as soon as it's clicked, it disables all the other choices in the same tiddler. Here's how it might work:

:: Start
It's a dark and stormy night aboard the [[Orient Express|http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orient_Express]]. You can't sleep; something about the motion of the train disturbed you subtly. So instead you have elected to spend the night in the dining car, sipping coffee and perusing [[the newspaper]].

"Hello?" a female voice asks from behind you, perhaps from the entrance of the car.
* <<choice "Turn around">>
* <<choice "Keep reading">>

Since choices require more thought than your average link, it's a good idea to format them a little differently than regular links. One popular way to do it is to use bullets, but it's up to you.

Choices are classed choice in CSS, so you can change their formatting with the selector span.choice.
