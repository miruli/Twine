The <<mask>> Macro

The <<mask>> macro is very simple. All it does is disguise a link to a tiddler under a different name. The format of a <<mask>> invocation is:

<<mask "existing tiddler" "displayed link">>

It's useful, for example, if you need to link to a tiddler in two places, where you would capitalize them differently. Remember that normally, links are case-sensitive.

:: Start
It's a dark and stormy night aboard the Orient Express. You can't sleep; something about the motion of the train disturbed you subtly. So instead you have elected to spend the night in the dining car, sipping coffee and perusing the [[newspaper]].


:: A reminder
"<<mask "the newspaper" "The newspaper">>, sir," the conductor says firmly. "You've dropped it."

:: the newspaper
If you believe what you read, the world is quiet. The London Times' front page is awash in some lurid tale of a fellow named John Lee whose execution failed thrice, and thus proven innocent -- or at least innocent enough to serve a life sentence.

Notice how you can stick a macro right inside a regular sentence. That's going to come in handy later.
